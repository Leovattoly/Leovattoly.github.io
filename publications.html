Predicting Optimal Conditions to Reach Target Particle Morphologies in Emulsion Polymerization: A Reverse Engineering Approach

Leo Vattoly, Nicholas Ballard, Usue Mori, Jose A. Lozano

Applied Intelligence (Springer) — under major revision, 2026

Particle morphology is a critical property of emulsion polymers that dictates their application properties. This work proposes a reverse-engineering approach: a neural network framework that predicts the optimal polymerization conditions needed to reach a target particle morphology, replacing traditional trial-and-error methods.

Detection of Process Deviations in Emulsion Polymerization Using Early Time Series Classification

Leo Vattoly and co-authors

Under review, 2026

Online monitoring of semi-batch emulsion polymerization is formulated as an early time series classification problem, detecting process deviations while the reaction is still in progress. Across 300 evaluated configurations, MiniRocket-based classifiers exceed deep learning architectures in both accuracy and earliness while training about 25× faster, reaching 90.5% accuracy after only 23.5% of the batch time.
