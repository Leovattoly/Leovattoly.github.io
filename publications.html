Predicting Optimal Conditions to Reach Target Particle Morphologies in Emulsion Polymerization: A Reverse Engineering Approach
Leo Vattoly, Nicholas Ballard, Usue Mori, Jose A. Lozano
Applied Intelligence (Springer)
Under major revision · 2026

Particle morphology is a critical property of emulsion polymers that dictates their application properties. Achieving the desired morphology requires precise control over reaction parameters such as the temperature profile and monomer feed rates. This work proposes a reverse-engineering approach: a neural network framework that predicts the optimal polymerization conditions needed to reach a target particle morphology, replacing traditional trial-and-error methods.

Detection of Process Deviations in Emulsion Polymerization Using Early Time Series Classification
Leo Vattoly and co-authors
Under review · 2026

Online monitoring of semi-batch emulsion polymerization is formulated as an early time series classification problem, detecting process deviations while the reaction is still in progress. A hybrid static–dynamic input architecture combines multivariate reactor trajectories with known feed-rate and set-point profiles. Across 300 evaluated configurations, MiniRocket-based classifiers exceed deep learning architectures in both accuracy and earliness while training about 25× faster — reaching 90.5% accuracy after only 23.5% of the batch time. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first application of early time series classification to emulsion polymerization.
